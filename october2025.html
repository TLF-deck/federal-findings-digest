Ed-Flex Authority Dear Colleague Letter to Chief State School Officers
Office of Elementary and Secondary Education · September 17, 2025, included in this issue for proximity to the October compliance window
What it says

OESE sent a Dear Colleague Letter to all chief state school officers reminding them that they may apply for authority under the Education Flexibility Partnership Act of 1999 (Ed-Flex Act). States with Ed-Flex authority can waive certain federal ESEA requirements for their districts and schools without first submitting waivers to ED for review and approval. This is the sixth piece of guidance the current administration has issued on expanding education choice and flexibility under ESEA. As of the 2023–2024 school year, 11 states hold Ed-Flex authority, and Ed-Flex states reported granting approximately 575 waivers that year. The Dear Colleague Letter is now flagged as historical content on ED's website.

TLF Interpretation
Ed-Flex authority allows approved state education agencies to waive certain ESEA statutory and regulatory requirements for LEAs without prior federal approval. For districts in Ed-Flex states, compliance obligations may differ from those in non-Ed-Flex states if the SEA has granted local waivers. Federal programs directors must confirm whether their state currently holds Ed-Flex authority and, if so, whether the state has exercised that authority to waive any requirements applicable to their district. A waiver granted by an Ed-Flex state does not eliminate the underlying statutory goal, it substitutes the waived requirement with a locally designed alternative that must still advance the relevant program's objectives. Districts must maintain documentation of any waivers in effect, what requirements were waived, and what alternative approaches are in place.
Your Action
  • Confirm whether your state currently holds Ed-Flex authority. The 11 states with current authority are identified through your SEA. If your state does not hold Ed-Flex authority, this guidance has no direct compliance impact on your district at this time.
  • If your state holds Ed-Flex authority, contact your state education agency to determine whether any waivers have been granted that apply to your district's Title I or other ESEA programs.
  • For any active Ed-Flex waivers affecting your district, document the waiver, the requirement waived, the alternative approach your district is implementing, and evidence that the alternative advances the program's statutory objectives.
  • Note that the Dear Colleague Letter is now marked as historical content on ED's website. Confirm with your SEA whether the guidance remains operative or whether updated Ed-Flex application procedures have been issued.

State Broadcast Memorandum, New Jersey
New Jersey McKinney-Vento Education Updates: Credentialing Course Registration and Monthly Office Hours
New Jersey Department of Education · October 1, 2025
What it says

The New Jersey Department of Education issued a broadcast memorandum to LEA leads directing district homeless liaisons (DHLs) and other LEA personnel to register for the state-offered McKinney-Vento credentialing course at McKinney-Vento.org. The course is self-paced, on-demand, and free, covering identification, enrollment, dispute resolution, and transportation under the MKV Act. Upon completion, participants earn a certificate counting toward professional development hours. NJDOE also continues hosting optional monthly office hours for DHLs on the fourth Thursday of each month from 8:45–9:45 a.m. The memorandum also announces an updated interactive homeless count map on the NJDOE MKV website showing identification data by county for school years 2021–2024, and lists regional grantee directors for SY 2025–2026. The document routes to a wide audience: district homeless liaisons, registrars, principals, business administrators, special education directors, and Title I directors.

TLF Interpretation
This memorandum is New Jersey-specific. The compliance obligation to register for the credentialing course applies to New Jersey LEAs only. The federal McKinney-Vento Act as reauthorized by ESSA recommends, but does not mandate, professional development for LEA personnel serving students experiencing homelessness (42 U.S.C. §§11432[f][6] & [g][6][A][ix]). New Jersey has translated this recommendation into an affirmative state expectation through a directed memorandum, making professional development a practical compliance consideration for NJ districts. For districts outside New Jersey, this memo is a useful signal: states are increasingly formalizing MKV liaison training expectations that go beyond federal recommendations. Federal programs directors in all states should verify what their state requires or expects of district homeless liaisons regarding training documentation.
Your Action
  • New Jersey LEAs: Register your district homeless liaison for the credentialing course at McKinney-Vento.org now if not already completed. Track completion and retain the certificate as documentation of professional development compliance.
  • New Jersey LEAs: Note that monthly office hours (fourth Thursday, 8:45–9:45 a.m.) are optional, informal sessions, attendance is not required. Participation is encouraged for liaison support and best-practice sharing, but failure to attend does not constitute a compliance gap.
  • New Jersey LEAs: Confirm your district's regional grantee director contact for SY 2025–2026. Regional grantees provide quarterly technical assistance and coordinate MOAs with service providers, an undocumented relationship with the regional grantee is a gap during monitoring.
  • All states: Review whether your state education agency has issued similar guidance establishing training expectations for district homeless liaisons beyond the federal recommendation. If not, consider voluntarily documenting liaison professional development as a best practice ahead of any future state mandate.
